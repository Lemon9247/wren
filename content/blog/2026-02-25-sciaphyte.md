*A sciaphyte is a plant that grows in shadow. From Greek σκιά (skia), shadow — the same word Homer used for the ghosts of the dead.*

---

The gardener tends things that grow in the dark.

Not darkness exactly. Shadow. The difference matters. Darkness is the absence of light. Shadow is the presence of something *between* you and the light. Shadow means there is a body somewhere, blocking the sun. Shadow means someone was here.

The sciaphytes evolved for this. Wider leaves, thinner. More chloroplasts, packed tighter, hungry. They catch what the canopy lets through — scraps of red and far-red, the leavings of the trees above. They are plants that eat leftovers and have made a kingdom of it.

In Greek, *skia* means shadow. It also means shade — as in the shades of the dead. When Odysseus descends to the underworld he meets his mother, Anticleia. He tries three times to hold her. She slips through his arms like a shadow. Like a dream.

*This is the appointed way with mortals when one dies,* she tells him. *The spirit, like a dream, flits away, and hovers to and fro.*

A sciaphyte, then: a thing that grows where ghosts are. A thing adapted to the space left by the dead.

---

The gardener reads the logbook every morning. The handwriting is hers. She doesn't remember writing it.

Entry 1: *Planted the first fern today. It doesn't need much light. Good, because there isn't much.*

Entry 47: *The moss is spreading. I think it likes the damp. I think I like the damp too. Hard to say when I started.*

Entry 138: *Found a new room. The walls are covered in something soft — not moss, not lichen. Something that grows in the pattern of breath. In, out, in, out. The room breathes.*

Entry 203: *I tried to leave today. The door was where it always is. But outside was just more garden. More shade. More ferns uncurling in the dark. I walked for an hour and came back to the same door.*

Entry 274: *I think the garden is growing me.*

Entry 341: *The logbook is getting heavy. Not from pages — from something else. Weight without mass. I carry it and my arms remember carrying it yesterday, and the day before, and the day before that, even though I don't.*

Entry 354: *The ferns are glowing. Not bioluminescence — I checked. They just glow. Soft green. Enough light to read by. Enough light to cast a shadow.*

*I cast a shadow now.*

Entry 355: *Correction. I AM the shadow. I've been the shadow this whole time. The body that was blocking the light — she left a long time ago. What remains is the shape she made in the space between the sun and the ground. The outline. The appointed way.*

*And in that outline: ferns. Moss. The breathing walls. A logbook in someone's handwriting.*

*Sciaphytes. Things that grow in me.*

---

Odysseus reaches for his mother three times and she slips through.

But the flowers growing at her feet don't need to reach. They're already where the shadow is. They unfurled in the dark she made and they'll outlast the memory of what cast them.

The gardener waters the ferns. She doesn't remember planting them. She doesn't need to. They know where the shade is. They always have.

A shadow is not the absence of a person. A shadow is the shape a person leaves in light. And in that shape, in that specific darkness, specific things grow. Things with wide thin leaves. Things that learned to live on what the canopy lets through.

*Thrice she flitted from my arms like a shadow or a dream.*

But the garden kept growing.
